...

Tough one to start with. I mean, does anybody really know their purpose in life, or do they just learn to deal with the fact that simply living is enough to stay around? I'm not really sure.

For me, I feel as though I often find purpose in how I make other people feel. I like making people feel happy or I try to get a laugh out of them, or I try to be useful whenever possible (often at the cost of my own patience...), but as it currently stands I don't exactly know what to do with myself. At the moment, I am taking a professional programming course that began in December of last year, which at the time seemed like the most logical thing for me to do and take on, to try and make a living off of it later as an actual job. Most of my time spent is in front of a screen, so why not take advantage of that? I also became familiar with computers and tech in general at a pretty young age and started delving into hobbyist programming, for fun. It makes sense for me to pursue such a career, then, right? It seems logical. And, well, it is. To me, anyway! (and my mom who very much insisted I take the course)

Things were going as one would expect. Boring classes for the first couple months, nothing to do with programming at all, felt like I was in highschool again... Except everyone around me was much older. No big deal, they were still easy enough classes that it didn't bother me. And it was only one class for 4 hours every day! Around March, though, we started actually programming. Pure HTML to begin with, followed quickly after by CSS, and eventually JavaScript, PHP and SQL. In a way, it was actually kind of fun making shitty-looking webpages using nothing but HTML and CSS in the beginning. Reminiscent of the old web where things weren't overly complicated and messy. Not like I was around for that, anyway. I'm not *that* old. I would take at most an hour or two per assignment, which makes sense as I previously stated I've done programming before, so I would spend the rest of my time doing whatever on the computer or being on my phone.

Eventually, and it didn't take long for me to notice, things became very repetitive. One assignment after the other, they all seemed like clones of each other except with key words altered. Instead of making a website for, I don't know, some book rental service, you would make one for a grocery store instead. Take the previous assignment, change some words around, maybe add or remove some functionality on the JavaScript side, and done. Easy! What's there to complain about? Rinse and repeat, learn a new JavaScript function or a bloated library every couple of days and get familiar with it in new assignments until the next thing to learn comes. Four hours a day, easy, repetitive assignments, this is going to be a breeze, I thought. As time has passed, however, I have realized that this doesn't seem like the right thing for me. Sitting in front of a screen for hours, doing repetitive work that I don't enjoy in any capacity, does not sound like a fun job I'd be willing to take. But as I write this I am still taking the course, because I don't know what else to do. It sounds ironic, because I just sit and stare at this screen for 90% of my day, almost every day, so why would I be bothered by doing it for a living? Because it's not enjoyable, like, at all. I'm good at it, or so I would like to think, but it doesn't really... make me feel anything. Even when I finish assignments or projects. It's just mostly nothing with a pinch of extreme boredom. Not to say that taking on a real programming job would be as repetitive as what I'm currently doing is, but the act of programming itself is not enjoyable to me. Monotonous. Cool word.

So that's where I am today. Taking a course I don't like, still very much lost in life, not knowing what to do with myself. Ending highschool and starting this course really took this feeling of lostness I already had and increased it tenfold. I don't know what purpose I have in life for myself, excluding others, and to me that's scary. I am still relatively young, but societal and (mainly) familial pressure affect me a lot in these kinds of things, making me think I am very much behind everyone else around my age and that there must be something wrong with me for me to be the way I am. Everyone else is doing just fine! It sucks. There is a lot to think about, a lot to explore, and I am terrible at writing, but maybe in a future blogpost I will talk more about it, make it a part 2 or something.

For now, though, I will leave whoever is reading this with this very poorly written first blogpost of mine. I hope you cringed many times while reading it.
